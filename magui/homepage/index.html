Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is central to Paris 2024, hosting events like beach volleyball and framing the Seine River opening ceremony, symbolizing unity and Parisian heritage.

The Phryge

The Phryge, inspired by the Phrygian cap, is a historic French symbol of liberty and revolution, dating back to the French Revolution. Chosen as the Paris 2024 mascot, it embodies freedom, resilience, and the Olympic spirit, connecting France's rich history with the global celebration of sports.

Spectators

Paris 2024 will welcome global spectators with affordable tickets and public events, ensuring everyone can experience the Olympic spirit in iconic Parisian venues.

The Olympic Games

The Olympic Games are a prestigious international sporting event with ancient origins. They were first held in Olympia, Greece, in 776 BCE as a religious festival honoring Zeus. The ancient games featured events like running, wrestling, and chariot racing, attracting athletes from Greek city-states. These games continued for nearly 12 centuries until their decline under Roman rule in 393 CE.

The modern Olympic Games were revived by Frenchman Pierre de Coubertin in 1896, inspired by the ideals of ancient Greece. The first modern Olympics took place in Athens, featuring 14 nations and 241 athletes competing in 43 events. Since then, the Games have grown into a global phenomenon, including both the Summer and Winter Olympics, with participation from nearly every nation.

Key moments include the introduction of the Olympic flag and motto, the inclusion of women athletes (1900), and milestones such as the Berlin Games (1936), the first televised Olympics (1960), and the Munich tragedy (1972). Today, the Games symbolize unity and peace, showcasing athletic excellence and fostering cultural exchange.
